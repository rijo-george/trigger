# Trigger

An intention engine that lives in your MacBook notch. Tell it what you need to do and when. It fires at the right moment.

## Download

**[Download for Mac](https://github.com/rijo-george/trigger/releases/latest)** — Universal binary, signed & notarized. macOS 14+.

## How it works

1. **Hover the notch** — the panel drops down. Type what you need to do.
2. **Pick a trigger** — choose when: a time, an app, a location, or just "when I'm back."
3. **Forget about it** — the notch fires your intention at the right moment. Done or snooze.

## 12 Trigger Types

- **In...** — 5 min, 15 min, 30 min, 1 hour, 2 hours, tomorrow
- **At time** — pick an exact time with presets (end of day, lunch, 9 AM)
- **Daily** — recurring at a set time, weekdays only or every day
- **Open app** — fires when you switch to a specific app
- **Close app** — fires when you quit an app
- **Before you leave** — fires when you lock your screen
- **When I'm back** — fires when you return from idle or sleep
- **Arrive at location** — fires when you connect to a Wi-Fi network
- **Leave location** — fires when you disconnect from a Wi-Fi network
- **Plugged in / Unplugged** — fires on power state change
- **Battery low** — fires when battery drops below a threshold
- **Docked / Undocked** — fires when external display connects or disconnects
- **New download** — fires when a file appears in ~/Downloads
- **Keep in queue** — manual, stays until you act on it

## Why

Built on *implementation intentions* — a technique from cognitive psychology (Gollwitzer, 94 studies, 8000+ participants) that increases follow-through by 2-4x. When you attach a "when" to every "what," your brain stops carrying open loops.

## Privacy

No accounts. No cloud. No tracking. Data stored locally at `~/.trigger/data.json`.
